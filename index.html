Model reference

WINDMI Equations

WINDMI is a low-dimensional, nonlinear representation of energy transfer from the solar wind through the magnetosphere and into the ionosphere.

Model overview

The model represents the coupled magnetosphere–ionosphere system as an equivalent electrical network. Inductances, capacitances, conductances, and resistive elements describe energy storage and transfer among the magnetotail, central plasma sheet, field-aligned current systems, ionosphere, and ring current. Solar-wind conditions supply the time-dependent driving voltage.

Model background: NASA Community Coordinated Modeling Center — WINDMI 1.0.

Eight-state model

(1) \[ L\frac{dI}{dt}=vB_s(t)-V+M\frac{dI_1}{dt} \]
(2) \[ C\frac{dV}{dt}=I-I_1-I_{ps}-\Sigma V \]
(3) \[ \frac{3}{2}\frac{dp}{dt}=\frac{\Sigma V^2}{\Omega_{cps}}-u_0pK_{\parallel}^{1/2}\theta(I-I_c)-\frac{pVA_{eff}}{\Omega_{cps}B_{tr}L_y}-\frac{3p}{2\tau_E} \]
(4) \[ \frac{dK_{\parallel}}{dt}=I_{ps}V-\frac{K_{\parallel}}{\tau_{\parallel}} \]
(5) \[ L_1\frac{dI_1}{dt}=V-V_1+M\frac{dI}{dt} \]
(6) \[ C_1\frac{dV_1}{dt}=I_1-I_2-\Sigma_1V_1 \]
(7) \[ L_2\frac{dI_2}{dt}=V_1-(R_{prc}+R_{A2})I_2 \]
(8) \[ \frac{dW_{rc}}{dt}=R_{prc}I_2^2+\frac{pVA_{eff}}{B_{tr}L_y}-\frac{W_{rc}}{\tau_{rc}} \]

Solar-wind input and trigger functions

(9) \[ vB_s=\begin{cases}V_0+L_y|v_x||B_z|, & B_z<0,\\ V_0, & B_z\ge 0.\end{cases} \]
(10) \[ \theta(I-I_c)=\frac{1}{2}\left[1+\tanh\left(\frac{I-I_c}{\Delta I}\right)\right] \]

Project information

About This Tool

A browser-based interface for running the WINDMI model with OMNI solar-wind data.

Development of the web tool

The WINDMI Web Tool was developed through a collaboration among Dr. Soumyajit Dey and Dr. Edmund Spencer at the University of South Alabama, and Dr. Purbi Adhya, a former postdoctoral fellow in Dr. Spencer’s research group who is now a faculty member at Spring Hill College. The interface is designed to make the model easier to run, inspect, and compare across selected UTC intervals without requiring a local scientific-computing installation.

Dr. Dey developed the Python computational backend that supports the current tool workflow. Dr. Adhya contributed her experience applying WINDMI to geomagnetic-substorm identification and model evaluation, while Dr. Spencer provided longstanding model expertise, scientific guidance, and continuity across successive stages of WINDMI development.

Model lineage

The core WINDMI framework and its foundational model equations grew from work led by Dr. Wendell Horton and collaborators at the University of Texas at Austin. Dr. Horton supervised Dr. Spencer’s doctoral research, which focused on geomagnetic storms and substorms with WINDMI. Dr. Spencer subsequently continued working with the model for many years, extending its formulation and applying it to a broad range of storm, substorm, current-system, and space-weather studies.

Dr. Adhya later advanced the model’s use for substorm analysis during her postdoctoral appointment under Dr. Spencer. The current phase, led in part by Dr. Dey, carries that development forward through a Python-based computational implementation and this accessible web interface.

Institutional references: Dr. Edmund Spencer, University of South Alabama · Spring Hill College faculty directory.

Use and interpretation

This tool is intended for research exploration, education, and reproducible comparison of WINDMI runs. Users conducting publication-quality analysis should document parameter choices, data handling, and initial conditions, and should compare results with established reference runs.
